Goal

The goal is to beat the dealer's hand without going over 21.

Initial Deal

Each player starts with two cards. The dealer has one card face up (the upcard) and one card face down (the hole card).

Card Values

  • Number cards

    Worth their face value.

  • Face cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks)

    Worth 10 points.

  • Ace

    Worth 1 or 11 points, whichever benefits the player.

Playing a Round

  • Hit

    Take another card from the dealer.

  • Stand

    Keep your current hand and end your turn.

  • Double Down

    Double your bet, take one more card, and then stand.

  • Split

    If your first two cards have the same value, you can split them into two separate hands (requires an additional bet equal to the first).

  • Insurance

    If the dealer's upcard is an Ace, players can take "insurance," a side bet up to half the original bet, which pays 2:1 if the dealer has Blackjack.

  • Even Money

    If you are dealt an Ace and a 10-value card (totaling 21, a blackjack), and the dealer's upcard is an Ace, you can take "even money", 1:1 payout, instead of risking a push in the hope of a larger win.

Outcome Scenarios

  • Blackjack

    If you get a "Blackjack" (an Ace and a 10-point card) as your first two cards, you win 1.5 times your bet, unless the dealer also has Blackjack, in which case it's a "push" (tie).

  • Win

    If your hand is closer to 21 than the dealer's, you win your bet.

  • Lose

    If the dealer's hand goes over 21, all active players win their bets.

  • Push

    If your hand goes over 21, you "bust" and lose your bet, regardless of the dealer's hand.
